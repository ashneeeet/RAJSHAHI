Rajshahi's Hidden Gems

HISTORY

Rajshahi, located in northwestern Bangladesh, has a storied history rooted in the Pundra Kingdom. It flourished as a center of education, trade, and culture under the Maurya, Gupta, and Pala dynasties. During the Mughal era, it became a silk-production hub. Later, under British rule, it evolved into an administrative and educational center.























CULTURE

Rajshahi’s culture reflects a blend of Bengali traditions and historical influences. Known as the “Silk City,” its rich heritage includes crafts like silk weaving and terracotta art. Festivals like Pohela Boishakh and Durga Puja are widely celebrated. The city’s vibrant music, literature, and cuisine highlight its artistic and cultural depth.
